1. course description
this course guides fourth-year is students through the research process from topic conceptualization
to final thesis defense. students will develop skills in research design, literature review writing, data
collection methodologies, statistical analysis, and academic writing. by the end of the semester,
students will have completed and defended chapters 1 through 3 of their undergraduate thesis.
2. learning outcomes
lo 1: formulate a clear and researchable problem statement relevant to the field of information
systems.
lo 2: conduct a comprehensive review of related literature and synthesize findings.
lo 3: design an appropriate research methodology including data collection and analysis plans.
lo 4: write and defend chapters 1, 2, and 3 of an undergraduate thesis following apa 7th edition
format.

5. formatting guidelines
all written outputs must follow apa 7th edition formatting: 12pt times new roman, double-spaced,
1-inch margins, page numbers on upper right.
citations: all sources must be properly cited in-text and listed in the references section. plagiarism will
result in automatic disqualification of the submission.
manuscript submission: final thesis manuscript must be submitted both in soft copy (pdf via lms)
and hard copy (spiral-bound) before the defense date.
