1. course description
this course examines the ethical, legal, and social dimensions of information technology. topics
include professional codes of conduct, intellectual property rights, data privacy and protection laws
(including the philippine data privacy act of 2012), cybercrime legislation (ra 10175), social media
ethics, algorithmic bias, ai ethics, digital divide, and responsible technology development. students will
analyze real-world case studies and develop frameworks for ethical decision-making in the it
profession.
2. learning outcomes
lo 1: analyze ethical dilemmas in technology using established ethical frameworks such as
utilitarianism, deontology, and virtue ethics.
lo 2: apply philippine laws on data privacy, cybercrime, and intellectual property to real-world it
scenarios.
lo 3: evaluate the social impact of emerging technologies including artificial intelligence, surveillance
systems, and social media platforms.
lo 4: formulate an ethical decision-making framework applicable to professional it practice.

5. course policies
class discussion: ethical discussions require respect and open-mindedness. students must listen
actively and engage thoughtfully. personal attacks or discriminatory language will not be tolerated.
position papers: must be submitted in apa format, minimum 3 pages, with at least 5 credible
references. ai-generated papers without proper disclosure and critical analysis will not be accepted.
case studies: group case study presentations must include a clear identification of the ethical issue,
applicable laws, stakeholder analysis, and a recommended course of action.
