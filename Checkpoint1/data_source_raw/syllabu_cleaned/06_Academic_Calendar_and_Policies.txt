attendance policy
a student who incurs absences of more than 20% of the total class hours prescribed for the course
shall be dropped from the course and given a grade of dropped (drp).
for a 3-unit subject meeting 3 hours per week (54 hours/semester), the maximum allowable absences
is 10 hours (approximately 34 class sessions depending on schedule).
tardiness: a student who arrives more than 15 minutes after the scheduled start of class will be
marked late. three instances of tardiness are equivalent to one absence.
code of student conduct
students are expected to maintain the highest standards of academic integrity. cheating, plagiarism,
fabrication of data, and misrepresentation are serious violations subject to disciplinary action.
use of mobile phones during class is prohibited unless explicitly permitted by the instructor for
educational purposes.
students must wear the prescribed school uniform and id at all times while inside the campus.
any form of bullying, harassment, or discrimination against fellow students or faculty members is strictly
prohibited and may result in suspension or expulsion.
scholarship and financial assistance programs
ched scholarship (unifast): available to qualified undergraduate students. applications are
accepted at the beginning of each semester. requirements include a minimum gpa of 2.0 and no
failing grades in the previous semester.
dost-sei scholarship: for science and engineering students. scholars must maintain a qpa of at
least 2.0 and complete 8 hours of community service per semester.
institutional scholarship: students who rank in the top 10% of their year level may qualify for a partial
tuition discount. academic performance is evaluated at the end of each semester.
work-study program: qualified students may apply for the work-study program, which provides a
monthly stipend in exchange for 20 hours of work per week in designated campus offices.
library services
the school library is open monday to friday, 7:30 am to 6:00 pm, and saturday, 8:00 am to 12:00 nn.
students must present their school id to borrow materials.
borrowing policy: students may borrow a maximum of 3 books at a time for a period of 3 days.
overdue fines are php 5.00 per day per book.
online resources: students have access to the school's subscribed online databases including
jstor, proquest, and the philippine elib. login credentials are provided at the start of enrollment.
it and computer laboratory policies
students must log in using their institutional account. unauthorized access to other users' accounts is a
violation of the acceptable use policy.
food and drinks are strictly prohibited inside the computer laboratory. students who damage
equipment due to negligence will be held responsible for repair or replacement costs.
saving of personal files must be done on personal storage devices (usb) or cloud storage. the
laboratory computers are cleared at the end of each semester.
